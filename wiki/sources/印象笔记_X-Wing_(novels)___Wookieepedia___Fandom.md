***X-Wing*** is a series of novels written by [Michael A. Stackpole](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Michael_A._Stackpole "Michael A. Stackpole") and [Aaron Allston](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Aaron_Allston "Aaron Allston"), named after the [T-65 X-wing starfighter](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/T-65_X-wing_starfighter/Legends "T-65 X-wing starfighter/Legends"), the [starfighter](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Starfighter/Legends "Starfighter/Legends") of choice for the [Rebel Alliance](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic/Legends "Alliance to Restore the Republic/Legends") and [New Republic](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/New_Republic/Legends "New Republic/Legends"). The *X-Wing* series was envisioned as "Star Wars meets [Top Gun](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top_Gun "wikipedia:Top Gun")".[[1]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/X-Wing_(novels)#cite_note-TERC-0) The first four books by Michael Stackpole are collectively known informally as the [Rogue Squadron](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Rogue_Squadron/Legends "Rogue Squadron/Legends") series, as together they form a self-contained plot involving the members of Rogue Squadron. They relate the events of the New Republic strike to [retake](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Liberation_of_Coruscant_(Galactic_Civil_War) "Liberation of Coruscant (Galactic Civil War)") the [Imperial](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Galactic_Empire/Legends "Galactic Empire/Legends") capital of [Coruscant](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Coruscant/Legends "Coruscant/Legends"), and the machinations of [Ysanne Isard](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Ysanne_Isard "Ysanne Isard") to bring about the New Republic's defeat.

The following three books by Aaron Allston are known informally as the [Wraith Squadron](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Squadron "Wraith Squadron") series due to their focus on that squadron, and relate the New Republic's campaign against the Imperial [warlord](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord/Legends "Warlord/Legends") [Zsinj](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Zsinj "Zsinj"). The events of this trilogy lead directly into *[The Courtship of Princess Leia](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_Courtship_of_Princess_Leia "The Courtship of Princess Leia")*.

The next three books all serve as standalones. *Isard's Revenge* overlaps with the end of *[The Last Command](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_Last_Command "The Last Command")* and recounts the conflict between the New Republic and [Ciutric Hegemony](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Ciutric_Hegemony "Ciutric Hegemony"). Unlike the previous books in the series, it has many connections with the *[Star Wars: X-Wing Rogue Squadron](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars:_X-Wing_Rogue_Squadron "Star Wars: X-Wing Rogue Squadron")* comic series, and serves as an informal epilogue to some of the plotlines from that series.

*Starfighters of Adumar* is a standalone as well. Unlike the other books, it has very little connection to other [canon](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Canon "Canon"), and has an entirely self-contained plot. It tells of a New Republic mission to the neutral world of [Adumar](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Adumar/Legends "Adumar/Legends") to negotiate its entry into the New Republic.

*Mercy Kill* is set over 30 years after the previous books, and features both new and returning characters. It is a return to Wraith Squadron and the team investigates a corrupt general.
