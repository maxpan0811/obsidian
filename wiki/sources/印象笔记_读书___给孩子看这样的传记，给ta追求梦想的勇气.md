“童真与诗意相遇”

mom读书

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/FD90988A-227D-464F-979C-C257DD86B87B.jpg)

**The Man Who Walked Between the Towers 高空走索人。**这本书的主角是历史上最伟大的高空钢丝杂技演员菲利普•帕特，画家用完全纪实的笔法，详细描述了帕特是如何完成这次高空走索的：从帕特开始筹划，到和朋友一起混上尚未完工的顶楼，再到他在高空走索的过程，以及后来受到的法院的惩罚。

没有人能解释帕特为什么要冒死登上高达400米的高楼——没有奖金，没有奖杯，也不是要为谁争光，因为“他就是这样的人：看见三个球，就必须耍起来；看见两座大楼，就必须在楼顶走一走”。唯一的解释是，他喜欢高空行走的自由感觉，只有在高空行走，他才感觉“宁静、快乐，而且无比自由”，他是为自己的快乐冒险。

* 2004年凯迪克金奖获奖作品

1974年菲利普·帕特，独自走上当时世界上高的建筑——美国世贸大厦的双子星大楼。世贸大厦在美国“9·11”事件中，受到恐怖分子劫持的飞机的撞击，引发大火并坍塌。作家在纪念帕特的壮举的同时，也哀悼了这两栋大楼的消失。

**动画版**

《The Man Who Walked Between the Towers》 | 09'39''

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/B61B02D4-3950-450F-8868-FD37169EF651.png)**认识作者**![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/9EFC1F0E-7BD7-4B4D-A0C8-9B7E91721314.png)

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/54E3147D-88F6-466D-AA5F-0881CFEEBF26.jpg)

**Mordicai Gerstein莫迪凯·葛斯坦**，是一位深受欢迎的童书作家和画家，生长于美国洛杉矶，从小受到名家画作的熏陶，深深爱上了绘画。已有30多本书出版，近期作品有他为音乐家查尔斯·艾夫斯写的传记《查理听到什么》。

葛斯坦的作品呈现出宽广的视野和深厚的内涵，无论学龄前儿童或小学生，甚至成年人都适合来读。由他创作和绘画的《高空走索人》，荣获了2004年凯迪克金奖，给他带来了至高荣誉。葛斯坦现与妻子苏珊·哈瑞斯、女儿丽莎定居在马萨诸塞州的北安普顿。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/B61B02D4-3950-450F-8868-FD37169EF651.png)**先读为快**![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/9EFC1F0E-7BD7-4B4D-A0C8-9B7E91721314.png)

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/7F4EC416-F41A-4B6C-8DE3-9677E95BD23E.jpg)

****The Man Who Walked Between the Towers 高空走索人****

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/925B8703-A5F8-473C-A121-679CFD191BDA.jpg)

A young man saw them rise into the sky. He was a street performer. He rode a unicycle. He juggled balls and fiery torches.

一个年轻人，看着双子塔高耸入云。他是一个街道表演者，可以一边骑着独轮车，一边玩着抛球或抛火把的主意。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/4C68A55D-0316-4990-801D-96143D11C7F9.jpg)![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/E3CEE620-6341-4CE5-9545-688425B17360.jpg)

But most of all he loved to walk and dance, on a rope he tied between two trees.

但是他最喜爱的是高空走索，把绳子系在两棵树之间，走在上边，甚至可以做出某些舞蹈的动作。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/C7F989BD-9CFD-4F4A-898A-B964DC34E543.jpg)

He looked not at the towers but at the space between them and thought, what a wonderful place to stretch a rope; a wire on which to walk. Once the idea came to him he knew he had to do it! If he saw three balls, he had to juggle. If he saw two towers, he had to walk! That's how he was.

这位年轻人不是看着双子塔，而是两者之间的空间，他想着：这里多么适合弄一个绳索，在上面行走啊。只要想到一个主意，他就想必须执行！如果有三个球，他就想着变戏法。如果前面有高塔，他就想到上面，搭上条绳索，上去走走！没办法，天生如此。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/CAEAB158-924E-4D5D-A230-DC3DD64FC214.jpg)

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/6BF95B2D-92FE-4C63-BBD8-E30EA557CAEF.jpg)

Hadn't he danced on a wire between the steeples of Notre Dame Cathedral above his amazed home city, Paris? Why not there, between these towers?

难道自己没有在巴黎圣母院的尖塔上进行高空走索，就在他的家乡？为什么不试试呢，就在这两座尖塔之间？

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/81DE6941-10EF-4F13-974B-407B35A803B5.jpg)

Of course he knew that, as in Paris, the police and the owner of the towers would never allow it. You must be crazy! they would say. You'd fall for sure!

当然，他知道：在巴黎，警察以及圣母院管理者不会允许他这样做。“你一定是疯了！”他们保准会这样说，“不会允许你的。”

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/F07B96F1-FD5F-4764-9130-C7150F51B95B.jpg)

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/F0BBF96E-7A53-40F5-8B4D-AE8F9B50AB3A.jpg)

And so Philippe-that was the young man's name- began a plan to do it secretly. The buildings are not quite finished, he thought. Maybe if I dressed as a construction worker…

因此那个叫菲利普的年轻人，开始秘密执行一个计划。他想着：双子塔还没有建成，如果打扮成一名建筑工人的话……

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/F2DC8AA2-46B3-4991-B38D-89C0EEDE099E.jpg)

Early an August evening he and a friend entered the south tower.

八月的一个傍晚，菲利普和朋友溜进了双子塔的南塔。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/F68BA26A-0A47-46CD-B7B7-EB4FD6EDDC09.jpg)

They got a four-hundred-and-forty-pound reel of cable and other equipment into the elevator, took it to the unfinished top ten floors, and waited till nightfall when everyone had gone. Then they carried everything up one hundred and eighty stairs to the roof.

他们搬了重达440磅的电缆及其他设备，运进了电梯，最终到达未完工的楼层，等待夜晚降临，所有的施工人员都离开之后（再搭建绳索）。接着他们扛着设备，爬了180层楼梯，到了顶楼。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/F740D730-A168-462A-A506-E67D61C45F07.jpg)

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/0152503C-4FAF-4AD9-8534-B8915C63464D.jpg)

At midnight, on the other tower's roof, two more friends tied a thin, strong line to an arrow and shot it across to Philippe, one hundred and forty feet away.

午夜，在双子塔的另一头，菲利普的两三好友，在箭头绑上了一个虽细且长的绳索，射向远在一百五十英尺之外的菲利普。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/A0FE8371-0A9F-401E-891B-4111696B8333.jpg)

It missed, and landed on a ledge fifteen feet below the roof. Bad luck thought Philippe.

失败了，距离菲利普那边只有15英尺的距离。真糟糕！菲利普想着。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/43AFADC0-E740-4EB7-9B33-0297D6BA9268.jpg)

He crawled down to the ledge, over the sparking city, and got the arrow.

他冒险爬上楼的边缘，身后是璀璨的城市灯光，抓到了那支箭。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/D1331FBF-1C7B-49D7-816C-A5651DA6D91A.jpg)

To its line he tied a stronger line, which his friends pulled back to their tower.

他用更加结实的绳子，把朋友从双子塔的另一头射的绳索系在了一起。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/8656AD2B-DDCF-4DCB-A3C9-6B20902FB8E1.jpg)

To his end of the stronger line, Philippe tied the cable on which he would walk. It was seven-eights of an inch thick.

在那条更加结实的绳子尽头，菲利普系上了要进行高空行走的电缆，直径达七八英尺。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/50D07937-06BD-4130-B642-49E876D8EBE8.jpg)

His friends pulled the cable over to their tower, but it was s heavy that it slipped from Philippe's grip. The cable's middle plummeted toward the street-pulling the friends on the other tower to the very edge. Philippe, just in time, secured his end.

朋友们帮忙把电缆系在了双子塔的另一端，但电缆太重，在菲利普手中滑了出去。电缆的中间部分快要滑向另一端的朋友那里时，菲利普及时抓住了。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/1A302C0A-196F-4852-BDC9-FB44DEB353BA.jpg)

It took three hours to pull the cable back up. Frantically, as the stars faded, they tightened it between the towers. It was past dawn before they were ready.

花了三个小时才把电缆拖回原位。说实话，直到星光褪去，他们才把电缆系好，天亮之前终于搞定了。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/4C989510-BA35-4334-80FF-8979FE7E1C5B.jpg)

Philippe put on his black shirt and tights. He picked up his twenty-eight-foot balancing pole. All his life he had worked to be here; to do this.

菲利普穿上黑色紧身衣，拿起了长28英尺的平衡棍。就像之前的那样，高空行走。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/97BD6DE1-DA20-4B2B-9010-CAF926BA7D68.jpg)

As the rising sun lit up the towers, out he stepped onto the wire.

太阳升了起来，照在双子塔上，此时他要踏上未知的旅程。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/A744DA11-CE99-4211-8D2D-F48E8AFBDDAA.jpg)

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/C9695B9F-A9D6-4544-B8E9-0ECBE82BE6FC.jpg)

Out to the very middle he walked, as if he were walking on the air itself. Many winds whirled up from between the towers, and he swayed with them. He could feel the towers breathing. He was not afraid. He felt alone and happy and absolutely free.

菲利普走到中间时，他就像走在云端一样。许多风漩从双子塔之间穿过，好像与他共舞。他能感受到双子塔的呼吸，因此并不害怕，在高空中，虽然独自一人，菲利普却很快乐、自由。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/48C03880-D9D6-4469-AFBE-F4674C8275DF.jpg)

A woman coming from the subway might have been the fist to see him. " Look! Someone walking on a wire between the towers!"

一位女士，刚从地铁出来，马上就看到了空中的菲利普。“快看，有人在双子塔上走钢丝！！”

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/9F3EAD83-557E-40D0-9966-5E3308A0A778.jpg)

Everyone stopped and looked up. They gasped and stared. It was astonishing. It was terrifying and beautiful. A quarter of a mile up in the sky someone was dancing. Police saw it, too.

每个人都停了下来抬头看。他们屏气凝视，太惊叹了，既让人紧张又是如此的美丽。几百米的高空中，有人居然在舞蹈，警察也看到了。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/8FD87FC0-A27C-416D-9306-7C815615231F.jpg)

Officers rushed to the roofs of the towers. "You're under arrest!" they shouted throught bullhorns. Philippe turned and walked the other way. Who would come and get him? For almost an hour, back and forth, he walked, danced, ran, and knelt it a salute upon the wire.

警察冲到了双子塔的顶层。“你已经被捕了！”他们用扩音器喊道。菲利普就转到了另一边。谁来抓住他？一个小时过去了，他在绳索上有时走着、有时做舞姿的动作，甚至还单膝跪在上面。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/CBD119C6-9930-4CC2-BA31-0CF95B96C2B4.jpg)

He even lay down to rest. The city and harbor spread beneath him. The sky surrounded him. Seagulls flew under and over. As long as he stayed on the wire he was free.

菲利普甚至停了下来休息。整个城市和港湾，在菲利普的非凡举动下屏住呼吸。周围是天空，海鸥飞来飞去。当菲利普在绳索上时，他感觉自己是自由的。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/7EA4513C-B65D-45B4-8291-CC7C91D0AF82.jpg)

When he felt completely satisfied, he walked back to the roof and held out his wrists for the handcuffs.

终于满足了，他走向楼顶，此时一双手铐在等着菲利普。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/F8984D86-CF12-475B-8DA3-86F3FB7D58F8.jpg)

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/DA41A0F2-0B3D-4BAB-9BB7-E9D9F203EADC.jpg)

They brought him to court. The judge sentenced him to perform in the park for the children of the city.

警察送菲利普到法庭。法官判决他要在公园里为纽约市的孩子们表演。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/5BAF610C-A2B8-41F2-8A3C-72978700F241.jpg)

This he did happily…thought during his performance so some boys playing on his wire jerked it and Philippe fell……but caught himself.

他很高兴这样做……在表演时，有些男孩儿动了菲利普的绳子，差点儿掉下去，不过幸好抓住了。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/5EC527D5-BD76-448C-B779-4DFC69DBA3C0.jpg)

Now the towers are gone.

现在双子塔成为了历史。

![](.evernote-content/A61E4976-40BD-44F8-9E16-CC6AF07E96B3/771D7F22-8B6A-4CC1-8D49-893663C21D87.jpg)

But in memory, as if imprinted on the sky, the towers are still there. And part of that memory is the joyful moring, August 7, 1974, when Philippe Petit walked between them in the air.

但在记忆中，双子塔就像印在了空中，一直在那儿矗立着。记忆里有着那个令人欢快的早晨，1974年8月7日，菲利普·帕特，云中漫步。
