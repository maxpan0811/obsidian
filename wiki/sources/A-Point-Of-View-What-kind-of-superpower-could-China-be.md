# A Point Of View: What kind of superpower could China be?

**A Point Of View: What kind of superpower could China be?**

**BBC“观点”栏目：中国将会成为怎样的超级大国？**

### 原文简介：中国之崛起令西方人惶恐不安，甚至有了荒唐的“中国威胁论”。一名英国学者在BBC电台“A Point of View”栏目中发表了一番对中国之崛起的看法，后转为文章形式载于BBC官网。他独辟蹊径，拿中西方历史作比，分析两者不同的民族性格，从而得出自己的结论。且看下文。

### 译文：

**China is on course to becoming a superpower - but not in the way many expect, writes economist.**

**“中国正在沿着超级大国的方向迈进，但并不是以很多西方人想象的那种方式。”****Martin Jacques****在书作中写道。**

Beijing these days is positively throbbing with debate. It may not have the trappings of a western-style democracy, but it is now home to the most important and interesting discussions in the world.When I addressed an audience of young Chinese diplomats at their foreign ministry a year ago, it was abundantly clear that a fascinating debate is under way about what kind of foreign policy might be appropriate for the global power China is in the process of becoming.

**近日，中国无疑是国际舆论的焦点。尽管中国自身可能没有西方民主的外衣，国内自由舆论不过尔尔，但它目前却是全球最有吸引力的舆论焦点。早在一年以前，当我给一名供职于中国外交部门的一名年轻中国外交官听众回信时，就很清楚的知道一场受人瞩目的讨论已然展开，讨论的主题便是哪种对外政策最适于即将成为世界大国的中国。**

What will China be like as a superpower? You might think it is already - it is not.

**那么，中国将会成为什么样的超级大国呢？你或许会说中国已经是超级大国了。其实不然。**

Its military power is puny compared with that of the US. While America has 11 aircraft carriers, China only commissioned its first last month - based on, of all things, a Ukrainian hull.And its global political influence is still extremely limited.

**首先，中国的军事力量与美国相比是那么微不足道。美国已经有了11艘航空母舰，而中国却只有一艘，上月才开始交付海军开始服役，更何况这仅有的一艘航母还是由从乌克兰购进的前苏联航母改造而成。并且，中国的国际政治影响力也已然十分有限。**

![](attachments/9f62724395f20ff5.jpg)

**（ 图为中国航母）**

The only sense in which China is a superpower is economic - that is, its economy is already over half the size of the US economy and projected to overtake it around 2018, notwithstanding its reduced growth rate of 7%. But this is overwhelmingly a function of China's huge population. In terms of technology and living standards it lags far behind the US.

**经济是中国唯一能称为“超级大国”的方面了。尽管中国目前经济增长速度下滑至7％，它的经济却总量已经超过美国的的一半，并有望在2018年完全超过美国。但这其实很大程度上取决于中国超大的人口数量。至于在科技水平和生活水平上，中国仍然远不及美国。**

**So when we speak of China as a superpower, we are talking about the future.**

**由此来看，当我们把中国当作是超级大国来讨论的时候，我们说的不是现在的中国，而是未来的情况。**

A common reaction to the idea of China as a superpower is that it will be like the US - except worse. Worse because it is not a democracy, it has a communist government and because its people are not like us. I guess that gives some the jitters.In fact we should not expect China to behave in the manner of the US. It will be very different. And nor should we assume that it will necessarily be worse.

**人们总是想当然地认为中国将会成为类似于美国那样霸道的超级大国，甚至更加糟糕。因为相比于美国，中国还不很民主，它是社会主义国家，中国人的意识形态也与我们很不一样。我想这多少也让我们西方国家的人们有些神经紧张了。其实我们不该认为中国会走美国的路子，也不该觉得中国的情况必定会比美国更糟。中国的崛起之路会与美国相差甚远。**

Why will it be different? Because its history is so different. Articles about China's growing involvement with Africa - in terms of trade and investment - often talk of the "new colonialism".Beware historical ignorance. China has never colonised any overseas territories. Overseas empires were a European speciality, with Japan getting in on the act for a short while too.China could have colonised South East Asia, for example, in the early 15th century. It had the resources, it had enormous ships, many times bigger than anything Europe possessed at the time. But it didn't.

**为什么这么说呢？因为中国独特的历史。中国与非洲国家关系日益密切，不断增强与非洲的贸易往来，增加在非洲的投资力度。但我们西方文章的论调一向把中国的行动定义为“新殖民主义”。请不要忽略历史——中国自古以来都没有在任何海外国家有过殖民地。海外帝国向来是欧洲的专利，虽然日本也有过短短的一段殖民史（殖民台湾）。比如说，早在15世纪早期（明朝时），当时的中国有足够的财力，也有许多巨轮，体型大于欧洲任何船只好几倍，要是中国想殖民东南亚，早就动手了，但是它并没有那样做。**

That is not to say China ignored its neighbours. On the contrary. For many, many centuries it dominated them - as a result of its sheer size and far more advanced level of development. China's relationship with them was based not on colonialism but what we now know as the tributary system. It neither ruled them nor occupied them. Rather, in return for access to the Chinese market and various forms of protection, the rulers of tribute states were required to give gifts - literally tribute - to the Emperor as a symbolic acknowledgement of China's superiority.The tributary system comprised what we know today as East Asia, home to one-third of the world's population. It stretched from Japan and Korea to the Malay Peninsula and parts of Indonesia.It proved remarkably stable, lasting for at least 2,000 years and only coming to an end around 1900.

**但这并不意味着中国对自己的邻国视而不见。恰恰相反，中国曾经凭借着自身占据绝对优势的国土面积以及领先的发展水平，许多世纪以来都控制着周边国家。但中国的控制并非基于殖民主义，而是建立在附属国体系的模式之上。中国从未直接统治这些国家，更未侵占其领土。相反，中国对这些国家开放自己的国内市场，并为其提供多种形式的保护，以免这些国家被外敌入侵，作为回报，这些附属国需要定期向中国皇帝赠送礼物，即朝贡，以象征性地表示其对中国至高无上权利的臣服。古代中国的附属国体系范围包括了如今占世界人口三分之一的整个东亚。东至朝鲜半岛及日本，南至印度尼西亚的部分地区。这样的附属国体系显然十分稳定，它延续了至少两千年，直到20世纪才崩溃。**

The West and China share an important characteristic - they both believe they are universal, a model for all others. But the way they have interpreted this in practice has been entirely different. For Europe, and latterly the US, it meant projecting their power around the world, most spectacularly during the heyday of colonialism in the 19th and first half of the 20th Century, when a large part of the world found itself under European rule.We governed from afar, exported our ways of doing things, imposed our languages, our education, our religion and much else besides.

**其实西方和中国有这一个很大的共同点——两者都坚信自己是宇宙的中心，是其他国家的典范。但是两者却用完全不同的实践方法来诠释这一观念。对欧洲以及后来的美国而言，这意味着自己应该把自己的势力渗透到全球各地，19世纪和20世纪的殖民全盛时期便是很好的佐证，当时世界很多地区都在欧洲的统治之下。我们（欧洲人）远远的统治着海外殖民地，输出我们的做事方式，把我们的语言、教育、宗教等等都强加于被殖民地人民。**

The Chinese, in contrast, preferred to stay at home. They believed the Middle Kingdom, the old name for China, literally meaning the centre of the world, was the highest form of civilisation. So why step outside into ever darkening shades of barbarianism?The seven great voyages of Zheng He between 1405 and 1433 around the East and South China Seas and across the Indian Ocean as far as East Africa left no permanent mark - they were about demonstrating the glory of the Middle Kingdom rather than a desire to conquer. Those who left China to settle in South East Asia were seen as leaving civilisation and deserving of no support or protection by the Emperor.

**相反，中国人却更喜欢固守于自己的疆土。他们认为自己是中央大国，是世界地中心，有着最为灿烂地文明，所以为何要踏出国门，到那些尚文开化的黑暗蛮荒之地呢？在1405～1433年间，郑和七次下西洋，沿中国东南沿海地区，横越印度洋，最远驶及非洲东海岸。其远洋的目的在于宣扬泱泱中国之国威，而非有意征服这些地区。那些离开中国，定居东南亚的华人，则被认为驶背弃中华灿烂文明，得不到天子的任何支持和保护。**

![](attachments/16106376760fa260.jpg)

（郑和雕塑）

Compare that with the way in which Britain and France celebrated the heroes of their colonial expansion. Our cities are littered with statues and street names in their memory.

**与中国相比，想想我们英国人和法国人是如何庆祝凯旋归来的殖民“英雄”的吧。直到今日，这些殖民“英雄”的塑象已然充斥在城市的街头巷尾，许多街巷也以他们的名字命名，以表达怀念。**

![](attachments/541b1efef40593c5.jpg)

（欧洲哥伦布纪念碑）

There is another reason why the Chinese have tended to stay at home. The country is huge, diverse - and extremely difficult to govern. The overwhelming preoccupation of its rulers down the ages has been how to maintain order and stability and thereby retain power. It remains just as true today.Rather than look outwards, China's leaders look inwards. The exception was China's own continental land mass. Its expansion, rather than to the four corners of the world, was confined to its own continent.The most dramatic example was the westward march of the Qing dynasty from the mid-17th Century which, in a series of bloody and brutal wars, doubled the physical size of China.

**此外，中国地大物博，地区差异很大，给中央集权统治造成极大困难。因此，在当时中国君主的心中，如何维持泱泱大国本身的有序和安定，如何维持自己的集权，这才是绝对的当务之急，而非是将铁蹄踏向其他国家的领土。这一点也同样适用于今天的中国。当今的中国领导人同样更着重将心思花在国内。最大的意外，顶多是中国会将目光瞄准整个亚欧大陆，它的扩张至多也仅限于亚欧大陆的尽头，而非整个世界。****举例而言，17世纪中期，清朝西征开拓疆土，通过血腥残忍的一系列战争，使中国领土扩大了一倍。**

So what, you might ask, does all this history tell us about how China might behave as a great global power? A great deal.

**你或许会问，那又如何？所有这些史实难道能告诉我们中国成为超级大国后将会作何姿态吗？很大程度上是可以的。**

Europe, I would argue, has historically been an extremely aggressive and expansionist continent. Its own history has been characterised by seemingly endless wars which were then transplanted onto a global stage during the era of colonial expansion and world war. Military might, the projection of power around the world, and the desire if necessary by force to impose our way of life on others, have been fundamental to the European story.And it is not difficult to see how the US - itself the product of European overseas expansion and settlement - inherited these characteristics from us.

**我们很难反驳这一点——欧洲自古以来都极有侵略性，是扩张主义者。整个欧洲史就是一部没完没了的战争史，这种好斗的性格后也来也随着殖民扩张和两次世界大战而传向世界各地。军事力量，对世界的控制力，以及将自身生活方式强加于其他民族的意愿，这几乎是欧洲史的重要组成部分。而美国作为欧洲海外扩张和移民的产物，继承我们欧洲人的这些性格也不足为怪。**

China won't be like this. It is not in its DNA. Its rulers will be far less interested in seeking to dominate the rest of the world and far more concerned with keeping themselves in power. That is what ruling a country containing a fifth of the world's population obliges. When Xi Jinping becomes Chinese leader next month, his in-tray, as with Hu Jintao before him, will be overwhelmingly filled with domestic rather than foreign issues.In time China will certainly come to enjoy huge global power. It will be exercised, however, in a rather different way.

**中国则决不会如此。中国人的血脉中根本没有侵略好斗的成分。中国统治者对谋求统治世界不感兴趣，而更加在意如何维持自己在国内的中央集权。这是统治一个占世界人口五分之一的大国的不二选择。下月，中国新一届领导人就要上任了，同胡锦涛主席一样，在他的公文篮中，国内事务的数量还将以绝对优势超过国外事务。中国也可能会适时的享受一把作为超级大国的权利，但这实施起来却并非易事。**

The iconic form of western power has been military. Extraordinarily, the US today accounts for around half of global defence expenditure. Before, European colonial expansion was only possible because its fighting capacity was massively superior to that of the rest of the world.That kind of overweening military power has never really been a Chinese characteristic.

**西方势力的标志是军事。尤其是美国，其军事防御开支占全球各国之和的一半。在此之前，欧洲的殖民扩张是唯一的可能，因为其战斗能力远远强于世界其他地区。这种傲视全球的军事力量从来没有出现在中华民族的特性中。**

![](attachments/1be58eebfc22b282.jpg)

Instead the quintessential forms of Chinese power will be economic and cultural. Over time, China's economic strength - given the size of its population - will be gigantic, far greater than that of the US at its zenith. Already, even at its present low level of development, China is the main trading partner of a multitude of countries around the world. And with economic power will come commensurate political power and influence. China will, if it wishes, be able to bend many other countries to its will.

**反之，中国作为超级大国的核心力量在于经济和文化。鉴于中国庞大的人口，随着时代发展，中国的经济力量将会更加强大，远远胜于美国经济全盛时期。甚至在当下，中国尚是欠发达的发展中国家，便已经成为世界上众多国家的主要贸易伙伴。与经济地位相伴而生的，便是同样的政治地位和影响力的。只要中国希望如此，它将可以令许多国家服从自己的意志。**

Cultural power will also be important to the Chinese. Theirs is a remarkable civilisation - having enjoyed a place in the sun not once but several times. During the Tang dynasty, for instance, from the 7th to the 10th Century, and most remarkably during the Song dynasty from the 10th to the 13th Century, with major advances in a host of fields from biology and hydraulic engineering to architecture, medicine, mathematics and cartography.

**文化力量对中国而言亦相当重要。他们有着灿烂的文明——历史上好多时期都是世界经济文化之中心，地位显要。比如说盛世唐朝（约公元7～10世纪），繁华的宋朝（约公元10～13世纪），那时的中国在生物学、水利工程、建筑、医药、数学和地图绘制等诸多领域都是世界的佼佼者。**

The Chinese are enormously proud of their historical achievements. They believe that theirs is the greatest civilisation there has ever been.They have a strong sense of their own superiority rooted in history. They have long had a hierarchical view of the world, with China at the top. And the rise of China is likely to accentuate these views.

**中国人向来对祖国的灿烂历史引以为傲。他们坚信自己的文明是最为悠久灿烂，无与伦比的。他们有种源自历史因素的强烈的优越感。长久以来，在中国人的观念中就有这样的等级观念——中国处在世界至高点。而当下中国之崛起就好像恰恰印证了这句话。**

But don't expect the Chinese to be impatient about their rise. In 1972 Henry Kissinger is reputed to have asked Zhou En Lai, the former Chinese premier, what he thought of the French Revolution. Zhou En Lai's response: "It's too early to know".The Chinese have a completely different conception of time to Westerners. Whereas Americans think very short, the Chinese think very long.For them a century is nothing.

**但不要以为中国对自己的复兴之路很没耐心。1972年，相传美国前国务卿基辛格曾经在访华期间问过周恩来怎么看待法国大革命。周恩来回答说****：“现在还难说呢。”****中国人与西方人的有着完全不一样的时间观念。虽然****有的时候美国人觉得很短的一段时间，中国会觉得很长（有政治隐喻），但****其实对中国人来说，一个世纪很短，不过眨眼功夫罢了。**
