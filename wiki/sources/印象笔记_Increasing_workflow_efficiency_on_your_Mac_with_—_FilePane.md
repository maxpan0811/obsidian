[**FilePane**](http://mymixapps.com/filepane) — a small, but versatile file management utility, that works via a drag-and-drop pattern.

How does it work?
-----------------

[FilePane](http://mymixapps.com/filepane) lives in your menu bar and can be triggered either by a dragging event or a customizable shortcut.

The “drop here” window appears near your cursor, as soon as you start to drag.

![](.evernote-content/1AB29EC2-2FB5-47E8-9DB6-FC9D997FF25D/BA7246F0-155E-4B0D-AB82-2EDF87661D69.gif)

![](.evernote-content/1AB29EC2-2FB5-47E8-9DB6-FC9D997FF25D/4639EE8C-EC02-4DC6-96D9-5C205C4B0658.gif)

If your intention is not [FilePane](http://mymixapps.com/filepane) related, you just drag the file further and the drop area disappers.

The same *auto-hide feature* applies to the main action windows. You just move the cursor away, and it vanishes.

![](.evernote-content/1AB29EC2-2FB5-47E8-9DB6-FC9D997FF25D/7785786D-A83E-4564-9F5C-C55F1F942576.gif)

![](.evernote-content/1AB29EC2-2FB5-47E8-9DB6-FC9D997FF25D/0DB73BBA-E0F1-44BF-9702-5124D99E0945.gif)

Window hides if the cursor is move away

As a result, [FilePane](http://mymixapps.com/filepane) never obscures your other apps or files and never gets in your way.

Moreover, there’s a *customizable exclusion list*, to illiminate the unnecessary ‘drop here’ popups. I suggest adding image editing apps to it (Photoshop, Pixelmator, Sketch)

**The app has a minimalist, simple, yet functional UI, which blends in well with Yosemite.** If you’re not fond of 10.10's new looks, [FilePane](http://mymixapps.com/filepane) has a slightly different look on Mavericks and works smoothly on both.

What can I do with it?
----------------------

Based on the file type you’ve drag-and-dropped, it offers numerous actions:

* crop, rotate, resize, convert and compress images on the fly;
* freehand drawing is available for images or screenshots;
* content sharing (e-mail, Twitter, Facebook) and Airdrop;
* set, store desktop pictures;
