First, make sure that the date picker in the tool bar is set to the past week or the past 30 days so that all your information is there. Now let’s have a look at it:    These are the window's main components:   * The **tab bar.** It lets you select different screens:   + **Overview.** Gives you a quick glimpse of how you spent your time and how productive you were.   + **Review.** Lets you account for all the time in your day, ensuring that everything is associated     with the right projects. We covered this in lesson 2.   + **Details.** Shows more in-depth information on individual activities.   + **Reports.** Lets you format and export data for further processing. * The **tool bar.** It has controls to view the project list, create a new task (available in Timing Professional), change the current date range, and filter or search your activities. * The **main area.** In Overview mode, this area shows you how much time you spent across different projects and apps, and how productive you are.   The main section, though, is all about your **Productivity score.** This is to help you make the most of your time, but it won’t automatically assign itself. To make your productivity score relevant, we’ll need to go back and edit some projects. **Editing Projects** You can see from your Overview Screen so far how well you’re tracking time. It’s probably not that accurate yet, which means you need to do some refining. What if you need to remove a rule, or want to change a project's color or productivity score? That's where the project editor comes in. Double-click an entry in the project list on the Review screen:  Here you can do a variety of things, including changing the project’s **productivity rating**. It’s entirely up to you how you rate your projects’ productivity, and all projects start with a “neutral” rating. Timing uses the rating you assign to compute your productivity score on the Overview screen. So make sure to set an adequate rating whenever you create a new project to avoid wondering “Why does this thing always tell me I’m 50% productive?” later on.  You can also **archive a project** from that screen. Archived projects will have their rules disabled, and Timing will also hide them in task editors and task suggestions — for a less cluttered interface.  When you’re editing a project, you can also change the project's **keywords** as part of the rule system. If you want to make more complex changes to the project's **rule**, click the triangle next to *Rule Editor*. Before you do so, though, make sure to read[our in-depth article](https://eur04.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=https%3A%2F%2Ft.dripemail2.com%2Fc%2FeyJhY2NvdW50X2lkIjoiNzI0NjM2NSIsImRlbGl2ZXJ5X2lkIjoiNzg5ODA4MTk5NyIsInVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vdGltaW5nYXBwLmNvbS9oZWxwL3J1bGVzP19fcz11eHhqdmsyeXpwYnh0a2Jjb2E2a1x1MDAyNnV0bV9zb3VyY2U9ZHJpcFx1MDAyNnV0bV9tZWRpdW09ZW1haWxcdTAwMjZ1dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249R2V0dGluZytTdGFydGVkK3dpdGgrVGltaW5nXHUwMDI2dXRtX2NvbnRlbnQ9RGF5KzQlM0ErVGhlK092ZXJ2aWV3K1NjcmVlbislRjAlOUYlOTMlODgifQ&data=02%7C01%7C%7C2def79c1902641e7773408d731df6fe0%7C84df9e7fe9f640afb435aaaaaaaaaaaa%7C1%7C0%7C637032708041871126&sdata=WWshq1bMthWOLSLAw81gmFRf9kEHWjZtP3hHJlCZBDM%3D&reserved=0) on editing rules. **Conclusion**  * The **Overview** screen gives you a quick glimpse of how you spend your time. * Change the date range in the toolbar to see a broad overview of the past week, month or quarter. * Edit projects to change the **Productivity Score** for greater accuracy. * Review your productivity score regularly so it stays current with your goals. * **Archive a project** to disable its rules and hide it from the task editor.
